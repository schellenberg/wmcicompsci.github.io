About WMCI Computer Science Club [Meets in Room 220]

Programming Competitions

We practice once a week (Mondays's at lunch) in room 220. Anyone is welcome. The idea is to go through questions from previous competitions in order to improve your coding skills, and to see if you might be interested in trying a competition. We’ll mostly work with Python (and sometimes C++). It's a great place to bounce ideas off other people trying to solve the same problems you are.

Our meetups start in mid-October, and continue until after the last competition of the year is over (usually around the end of April).

Other Activities

A totally non-exhaustive list of things the CS Club members have done during our meetups include:

  • CS Circles (U of Waterloo) - a great self-paced introduction to programming, using the Python language.
  • CheckIO - a Python game. Mini-challenges to be solved using Python.
  • Project Euler - solve interesting mathematical problems algorithmically.
  • PicoCTF - high school hacking competition (capture the flag style).
  • HSCTF - another high school hacking competition (capture the flag style).
  • TopCoder - Runs a ton of computing competitions online. You earn points with your results and move up in the rankings.
  • RoboCode - create an AI tank and battle versus others. Java or .NET.
